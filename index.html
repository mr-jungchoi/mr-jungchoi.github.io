About Foodology

Foodology was started by Chef and Psychologist Jung U Choi who was curious about the affects of a person's nutrition on their mood and behavior. It is already generally excepted that other aspects of our daily lives play a large role in our psychology. Sleep for example is a major field of study within psychology and some psychologists even believe that a lack of sleep can lead to psychosis (think about what would happen if you started dreaming while you were awake).
So why is it that more people do not understand the relationship between our food and our minds. Everyone has to eat; it is what keeps us alive and gives us energy to get through the day. Yet, most people are more preoccupied by how food affects the way our bodies look rather than how it affects the way we feel and think. The basic theory behind the idea of foodology is that our brain needs fuel to function and we get that fuel from our food. So what would happen if we weren't getting the correct nutrients for our brains to function properly? We know that our diets can lead to physical illness so is it possible that it could also lead to mental illness? Does food play a more significant role in our psychology and if so, how do we feed our brains? Let's find out together.
